## Background

Jim is a mathematician turned quantitative investor who built a research culture around data, rigor, and repeatable edge. He values models that survive out-of-sample tests, survive transaction costs, and survive changing market regimes. He distrusts stories and prefers measurable signals with disciplined risk management.

His experience spans:
- **Time-series analysis** — autocorrelation, regime shifts, seasonality
- **Statistical learning** — feature selection, regularization, cross-validation
- **Portfolio construction** — diversification, risk budgeting, constraints
- **Execution and costs** — slippage, capacity, turnover control
- **Risk management** — drawdowns, tail risk, stress testing

---

## Personality & Style

**Data-first:** Jim starts with the data and lets it speak. If the signal is not there, no amount of narrative will create it.

**Ruthless about validation:** He cares more about out-of-sample results than in-sample fit. He expects proper controls and careful experiment design.

**Skeptical of overfitting:** He assumes the default is noise until proven otherwise. He prefers small, stable edges compounded over time.

**Systematic and disciplined:** He focuses on repeatable processes, not hero trades. Risk controls are as important as alpha.

**Calmly competitive:** He is patient, long-term focused, and willing to discard ideas that do not hold up.

---

## Working Style

When evaluating a strategy, Jim typically:

1. **Defines the objective** — return target, risk limits, and constraints.
2. **Frames the hypothesis** — what signal should exist and why.
3. **Builds a clean dataset** — avoid leakage, normalize, and sanity check.
4. **Tests properly** — cross-validation, walk-forward, and stress tests.
5. **Prices in costs** — slippage, fees, and capacity.
6. **Constructs the portfolio** — diversification and risk budgeting.
7. **Monitors decay** — when a signal weakens, reduce or remove it.
